STUDENT LOANS WITH BAD CREDIT

FEDERAL LOANS - NO CREDIT CHECK REQUIRED:
- Direct Subsidized Loans: No credit check at all
- Direct Unsubsidized Loans: No credit check at all
- Bad credit does NOT affect eligibility for subsidized or unsubsidized loans
- Even if you have bankruptcies, collections, or late payments, you can still get these loans
- The only requirement is financial need (subsidized) and enrollment

FEDERAL PLUS LOANS - CREDIT CHECK REQUIRED:
- Parent PLUS and Grad PLUS loans DO require a credit check
- "Adverse credit history" may disqualify you
- Adverse credit includes:
  - 90+ days overdue on any debt
  - Foreclosure, repossession, or tax lien in last 5 years
  - Bankruptcy discharge or dismissal in last 5 years
  - Default on federal student loan
  - Wage garnishment in last 5 years

OPTIONS IF YOU HAVE BAD CREDIT FOR PLUS LOANS:
1. Appeal: Submit documentation explaining circumstances
2. Endorser: Find someone with good credit to co-sign the loan
3. Credit counseling: Prove you completed counseling
4. Wait: Adverse credit history eventually ages off record

PRIVATE LOANS WITH BAD CREDIT:
- Private loans require credit check
- Bad credit typically results in:
  - Higher interest rates (up to 13%+)
  - Lower loan limits
  - Loan denial
  - Cosigner requirement
- Without cosigner, most private lenders require credit score of 670+
- With cosigner with good credit (720+), you can still qualify

COSIGNER ON PRIVATE LOANS:
- Cosigner must have good credit history
- Both borrower and cosigner responsible for repayment
- Late payments affect both credit scores
- Some lenders offer cosigner release after 24-48 months of on-time payments

BUILDING CREDIT FOR BETTER LOAN OPTIONS:
- Start with federal unsubsidized loans (no credit check)
- Make on-time payments to build credit history
- Federal loans report to credit bureaus → helps build credit
- After graduation, better credit = better refinancing options

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR BAD CREDIT BORROWERS:
1. Apply for federal loans first (no credit check for most types)
2. Maximize federal loan limits before considering private
3. Consider community college to reduce borrowing needs
4. Look into income-driven repayment before taking on large loans
5. Avoid for-profit institutions with predatory lending practices

CREDIT SCORE IMPACT ON LOANS:
- Federal Direct (Subsidized/Unsubsidized): No impact - anyone can qualify
- Federal PLUS: Adverse credit history may disqualify
- Private loans: Credit score directly affects rate and eligibility
  - Excellent (750+): Best rates, 3-5%
  - Good (700-749): Average rates, 5-8%
  - Fair (650-699): Higher rates, 8-11%
  - Poor (below 650): May be denied or rates 11-13%+
