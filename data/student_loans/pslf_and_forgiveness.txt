PUBLIC SERVICE LOAN FORGIVENESS (PSLF) - COMPLETE GUIDE

WHAT IS PSLF:
Public Service Loan Forgiveness is a federal program that forgives remaining student loan balances after 10 years of qualifying payments while working for a qualifying employer.

HOW PSLF WORKS - STEP BY STEP:
1. Work full-time for a qualifying employer (government or nonprofit)
2. Enroll in an income-driven repayment plan
3. Make 120 qualifying monthly payments (10 years)
4. Submit Employment Certification Form annually
5. Apply for forgiveness after 120 payments
6. Remaining balance is forgiven TAX-FREE

QUALIFYING EMPLOYERS:
- Federal, state, local, or tribal government organizations
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
- Other nonprofits providing qualifying public services
Examples:
  - Public schools and universities
  - Public hospitals
  - Military service
  - AmeriCorps and Peace Corps
  - Public libraries
  - Law enforcement agencies

NOT QUALIFYING EMPLOYERS:
- For-profit companies (even if working in public service role)
- Partisan political organizations
- Labor unions
- Nonprofit organizations that do not have 501(c)(3) status
  (unless they provide qualifying public services)

QUALIFYING PAYMENTS:
- Must be on income-driven repayment plan (IBR, PAYE, REPAYE, ICR)
- Must be made on time (within 15 days of due date)
- Must be for the full amount shown on billing statement
- Must be made while working full-time for qualifying employer
- Payments do NOT need to be consecutive
- $0 payments on income-driven plans count as qualifying payments

ELIGIBLE LOANS FOR PSLF:
- Only Direct Loans qualify
- FFEL (Federal Family Education Loans) do NOT qualify
- Perkins Loans do NOT qualify directly
- Solution: Consolidate FFEL/Perkins into Direct Consolidation Loan
  (Note: Consolidation resets payment count to zero)

TAX TREATMENT OF PSLF:
- PSLF forgiveness is NOT taxable income
- This is different from income-driven repayment forgiveness
  (IDR forgiveness IS potentially taxable after 20-25 years)

120 PAYMENT REQUIREMENT DETAILS:
- Payments must be made monthly (12 per year)
- 120 payments = exactly 10 years of payments
- Payments do not need to be on the same loan
- Can change employers as long as both qualify
- Can switch income-driven plans during the 10 years

ANNUAL CERTIFICATION (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED):
- Submit PSLF Employment Certification Form annually
- Verifies employer qualifies before you make all 120 payments
- Confirms payments are counting toward the 120
- Prevents surprises at year 10
- File at StudentLoans.gov

COMMON PSLF MISTAKES:
- Not being on income-driven repayment plan
- Working for non-qualifying employer
- Having FFEL loans (must consolidate first)
- Not submitting annual employment certification
- Missing payments or paying wrong amount
- Applying before 120 payments are complete

INCOME-DRIVEN REPAYMENT + PSLF COMBINATION:
- Income-driven plans reduce monthly payments
- Lower payments = less out-of-pocket over 10 years
- Remaining balance (often large) forgiven tax-free after 120 payments
- Best strategy for high-balance, lower-income borrowers

PAYMENT AMOUNT EXAMPLE ($50,000 loan, PSLF path):
- Income-driven payment: ~$200/month
- Total paid over 10 years: ~$24,000
- Remaining balance forgiven: ~$40,000-$60,000 (with interest)
- Tax on forgiveness: $0

VERSUS standard repayment on same loan:
- Standard payment: ~$590/month
- Total paid over 10 years: ~$70,800
- Nothing forgiven

PSLF APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Verify 120 payments made using Payment Count on StudentLoans.gov
2. Confirm qualifying employment for all periods
3. Submit PSLF Application (PSLF Form)
4. MOHELA (loan servicer) reviews application
5. Receive forgiveness decision (typically 6-12 weeks)
6. Remaining balance is forgiven

TEACHER LOAN FORGIVENESS (DIFFERENT FROM PSLF):
- Forgives up to $17,500 for teachers in low-income schools
- Only 5 years required (not 10)
- Cannot receive both Teacher and PSLF forgiveness for same period
- Must choose one or use them sequentially
