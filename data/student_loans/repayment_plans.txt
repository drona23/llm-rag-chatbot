FEDERAL STUDENT LOAN REPAYMENT PLANS

STANDARD 10-YEAR REPAYMENT PLAN
- Duration: 10 years (120 payments)
- Monthly payment: Fixed amount
- Example: $30,000 loan at 6.53% = $357/month
- Total interest paid: ~$12,960
- Best for: Those who can afford moderate payments
- Advantages: Lowest total interest paid, quickest payoff
- Disadvantages: Highest monthly payment
- Eligibility: All Direct Loan borrowers

GRADUATED REPAYMENT PLAN
- Duration: 10 years (120 payments)
- Monthly payment: Starts low, increases every 2 years
- Example: $30,000 loan starts at $150/month, increases to $540/month
- Total interest: Slightly higher than Standard Plan
- Best for: Recent graduates with expected income growth
- Advantages: Lower initial payments, built-in payment increase
- Disadvantages: Payments increase, higher total interest
- Eligibility: All Direct Loan borrowers

INCOME-DRIVEN REPAYMENT PLANS:

1. INCOME-BASED REPAYMENT (IBR)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income (new borrowers) or 15% (old borrowers)
- Duration: 20-25 years
- Loan forgiveness: After 20-25 years, remaining balance forgiven
- Income verification: Required annually
- Best for: Low-income borrowers, large loan amounts
- Tax consequence: Forgiven amount may be taxable income

2. INCOME-CONTINGENT REPAYMENT (ICR)
- Payment: Higher of 20% of discretionary income OR fixed 12-year payment
- Duration: 25 years
- Loan forgiveness: After 25 years, remaining balance forgiven
- Available to: All Direct Loan borrowers (including PLUS loans)
- Recalculated: Annually based on income

3. INCOME-SENSITIVE REPAYMENT
- Payment: Based on annual income (not percentage of discretionary income)
- Duration: Up to 15 years
- No loan forgiveness
- For: FFEL loans (older federal loans)
- Monthly payment example: $100-$500 depending on income

4. PAY AS YOU EARN (PAYE)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income
- Duration: 20 years
- Loan forgiveness: After 20 years
- Best for: Low-income borrowers, high-balance loans
- Income verification: Required annually
- Tax consequence: Forgiven amount may be taxable
- Eligibility: Relatively recent loan recipients

5. REVISED PAY AS YOU EARN (REPAYE)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income
- Duration: 20 years (undergrad) or 25 years (grad)
- Loan forgiveness: After 20-25 years
- Available to: All Direct Loan borrowers (including PLUS)
- Interest subsidy: Government pays some unsubsidized interest if payment < accrued interest
- Most recent plan with best benefits

MONTHLY PAYMENT COMPARISON ($30,000 Loan at 6.53%):
- Standard 10-year: $357/month fixed
- Graduated: $150-$540/month (increases)
- IBR (10% discretionary): $100-$400/month (varies by income)
- PAYE (10% discretionary): $100-$400/month (varies by income)
- ICR (20% discretionary): $150-$500/month (varies by income)

LOAN FORGIVENESS PROGRAMS:

PUBLIC SERVICE LOAN FORGIVENESS (PSLF)
- For: Government/nonprofit employees
- Duration: 10 years (120 qualifying payments)
- Remaining balance: Forgiven after 120 payments
- Income-driven plan: Required
- Requirements: Work for eligible employer, make on-time payments
- Tax consequence: Forgiven amount is NOT taxable

TEACHER LOAN FORGIVENESS
- For: Teachers in low-income schools
- Amount: Up to $17,500 forgiveness
- Duration: 5 years of teaching required
- No forgiveness after 5 years (one-time benefit)

PERKINS LOAN FORGIVENESS
- For: Teachers, nurses, military, public service workers
- Amount: Up to 100% forgiveness
- Duration: Varies by profession (typically 5 years)

CONSOLIDATION LOAN NOTE:
- Consolidation does NOT directly lead to forgiveness
- But consolidation required for some forgiveness programs
- Extends repayment (increases total interest paid)
- Benefits: Single payment, potential for income-driven plans
