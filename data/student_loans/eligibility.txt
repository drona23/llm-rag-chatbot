FEDERAL STUDENT LOAN ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

BASIC REQUIREMENTS FOR ALL FEDERAL LOANS:
- Be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen
- Have a valid Social Security Number
- Have a high school diploma or GED (or pass ability-to-benefit test)
- Be enrolled at least half-time in an eligible degree program
- Not be in default on another federal student loan
- Not owe overpayment on federal student aid
- Maintain satisfactory academic progress (SAP)

FAFSA (FREE APPLICATION FOR FEDERAL STUDENT AID):
- Required to apply for all federal student loans
- Determines Expected Family Contribution (EFC)
- Opens October 1 each year
- Deadline: June 30 (but apply early for better aid)
- Financial need determined by: Cost of Attendance - EFC
- Assets considered: Student/parent savings, income, investments

DIRECT SUBSIDIZED LOANS - ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Must demonstrate financial need
- FAFSA required
- Can only borrow up to financial need amount
- Must be undergraduate student (grad students not eligible)
- Grade level limits:
  - 1st year: $3,500 max
  - 2nd year: $4,500 max
  - 3rd+ year: $5,500 max

DIRECT UNSUBSIDIZED LOANS - ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- NO financial need requirement
- FAFSA still required (for federal aid consideration)
- Available to undergrad and graduate students
- Higher borrowing limits than subsidized
- Graduate students can borrow up to cost of attendance

DIRECT PLUS LOANS - ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Parent PLUS: Parent of dependent undergraduate student
- Graduate PLUS: Graduate or professional degree student
- Credit check required (some adverse credit history may disqualify)
- Cannot have adverse credit history including:
  - 90+ days past due on any debt
  - Foreclosure in past 5 years
  - Bankruptcy discharge/dismissal in past 5 years
  - Default on federal student loan
  - Wage garnishment
- Note: Explanatory statement can be submitted if adverse credit exists

INELIGIBLE STUDENTS:
- Non-citizen/non-eligible immigrant
- No high school diploma/GED
- Full-time enrollment not maintained
- Failed to make Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
- Enrolled in non-accredited program
- In default on federal loan
- Owing overpayment on federal aid
- Incarcerated individuals (for certain loan types)

CITIZENSHIP/LEGAL STATUS REQUIREMENTS:
Eligible:
- U.S. citizen
- U.S. national
- U.S. permanent resident
- F-1 student visa holders (some restrictions)
- J-1 exchange student visa holders (some restrictions)

Not Eligible:
- Undocumented immigrants
- F-2 dependent visa holders
- Tourist/visitor visa status
- Work visa holders (H-1B, L-1, etc.) - generally not eligible

INCOME LIMITS FOR DEPENDENT vs INDEPENDENT STATUS:
Dependent Status (most undergrads):
- Parents' income/assets considered for FAFSA
- Even if no parental contribution expected
- Can't file as independent just to avoid reporting parents' info

Independent Status:
- You are 24+ years old, OR
- Married, OR
- Graduate/professional student, OR
- Have legal dependents other than spouse, OR
- Orphaned/in foster care, OR
- Determined by school to have special circumstances

SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP):
- Maintain minimum GPA (typically 2.0 or higher)
- Pass minimum credits per term (typically 75% of attempted)
- Complete degree within maximum timeframe (150% of program length)
- Reassessed each term
- Loss of eligibility if SAP not met

SCHOOL-SPECIFIC ELIGIBILITY:
- School must be accredited by recognized accrediting agency
- School must participate in federal student aid programs
- School must be eligible institution:
  - Colleges/universities
  - Community colleges
  - Career/technical schools
  - Some online programs
  - Some for-profit schools

NOT ELIGIBLE:
- Pre-K programs
- Some online-only certificate programs
- Non-accredited institutions
- Some school districts/high schools

INCOME REQUIREMENT FOR INCOME-DRIVEN PLANS:
- No minimum income required to qualify
- Some people with low income may have $0 payment
- Discretionary income: (Adjusted Gross Income) - (Poverty Guideline)
- Must recertify annually
