FEDERAL STUDENT LOAN TYPES

1. DIRECT SUBSIDIZED LOANS
- For undergraduate students with financial need
- Government pays interest while in school (at least half-time)
- Interest rate: 6.53% (2024-2025 academic year)
- Minimum payment: $0 while in school
- Maximum borrowing: $3,500-$5,500 per year (depends on year in school)
- Grace period: 6 months after graduation before repayment begins
- Total lifetime limit: $23,000

2. DIRECT UNSUBSIDIZED LOANS
- Available to undergraduate and graduate students
- Student responsible for all interest (even while in school)
- Interest rate: 7.05% (2024-2025 academic year)
- Can defer payments while in school, but interest accrues
- Undergraduate max: $5,500-$20,500 per year
- Graduate max: $20,500 per year
- Total lifetime limit: $57,500 (including subsidized loans)
- Grace period: 6 months after graduation

3. FEDERAL PLUS LOANS (Parent PLUS & Grad PLUS)
- Parent PLUS: Available to parents of dependent undergraduates
- Grad PLUS: Available to graduate and professional students
- Interest rate: 8.05% (2024-2025 academic year)
- No annual borrowing limit (within cost of attendance)
- Requires credit check (some restrictions if adverse credit history)
- Repayment begins 60 days after final disbursement
- Can defer payments while student is enrolled at least half-time
- No grace period

4. DIRECT CONSOLIDATION LOANS
- Combines multiple federal student loans into one
- Single monthly payment instead of multiple
- Interest rate: weighted average of consolidated loans (rounded up)
- Can extend repayment period (up to 30 years)
- No origination fee
- Eligible loans: Direct Loans, Federal Family Education Loans (FFEL), Perkins Loans

5. PERKINS LOANS (Limited availability)
- Low-interest federal loans for students with exceptional financial need
- Interest rate: 5% (fixed)
- Interest doesn't accrue while in school or during grace periods
- Maximum: $5,500 per year (undergrad), $8,000 per year (graduate)
- 9-month grace period after graduation
- Repayment period: up to 10 years
- Note: New Perkins loans may not be available at all schools

PRIVATE STUDENT LOANS
- Offered by banks, credit unions, and online lenders
- Interest rates vary: 3.0%-13.0%+ (depends on creditworthiness, lender)
- Require credit check or cosigner
- Fewer borrower protections than federal loans
- No grace period on some loans
- Repayment typically begins while in school
- Examples: Discover Student Loans, SoFi, LendingClub
