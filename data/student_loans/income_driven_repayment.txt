INCOME-DRIVEN REPAYMENT (IDR) PLANS - COMPLETE GUIDE

WHAT IS INCOME-DRIVEN REPAYMENT:
Income-driven repayment plans set your monthly student loan payment as a percentage of your discretionary income, instead of a fixed amount. Payments adjust annually based on your income and family size.

DISCRETIONARY INCOME DEFINITION:
Discretionary Income = Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) - Poverty Guideline
- Poverty Guideline varies by family size and state
- Example: Single person in 2024: poverty level = ~$15,060
- If your AGI = $40,000, discretionary income = $40,000 - $15,060 = $24,940
- Monthly payment (10%) = $24,940 × 10% ÷ 12 = ~$208/month

THE 4 INCOME-DRIVEN PLANS:

1. INCOME-BASED REPAYMENT (IBR)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income (new borrowers after July 2014)
          15% of discretionary income (borrowers before July 2014)
- Duration: 20 years (new borrowers) or 25 years (older borrowers)
- Forgiveness: Remaining balance forgiven after repayment period
- Tax: Forgiven amount may be taxable income
- Cap: Payment never exceeds Standard 10-year payment amount
- Eligibility: Must have financial hardship

2. PAY AS YOU EARN (PAYE)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income
- Duration: 20 years
- Forgiveness: Remaining balance after 20 years
- Tax: Forgiven amount may be taxable income
- Cap: Payment never exceeds Standard 10-year payment amount
- Eligibility: New borrowers as of Oct 1, 2007 with loans after Oct 1, 2011
- Best for: Recent borrowers with high debt relative to income

3. REVISED PAY AS YOU EARN (REPAYE)
- Payment: 10% of discretionary income
- Duration: 20 years (undergrad loans) or 25 years (grad loans)
- Forgiveness: Remaining balance after repayment period
- Tax: Forgiven amount may be taxable income
- No payment cap (can exceed Standard plan payment if income rises)
- Interest subsidy: Government pays 50% of unpaid interest on subsidized loans
- Eligibility: All Direct Loan borrowers (no hardship required)
- Best for: Those who want PSLF; most generous interest subsidy

4. INCOME-CONTINGENT REPAYMENT (ICR)
- Payment: Lower of: 20% of discretionary income OR fixed 12-year payment
- Duration: 25 years
- Forgiveness: Remaining balance after 25 years
- Tax: Forgiven amount may be taxable income
- Only income-driven plan available to Parent PLUS Loan borrowers
  (after consolidation into Direct Consolidation Loan)
- Eligibility: All Direct Loan borrowers

HOW ANNUAL RECERTIFICATION WORKS:
- Every year you must recertify income and family size
- Submit tax return or income documentation
- Payment recalculated based on new income
- If income rises, payment rises
- If income falls, payment falls
- If you miss recertification, you're removed from IDR plan temporarily

$0 PAYMENT POSSIBILITY:
- If your income is at or below 150% of poverty guideline, payment = $0
- $0 payments still count toward forgiveness
- Interest still accrues on unsubsidized loans during $0 payments
- REPAYE subsidizes 50% of unpaid interest even at $0 payment

FORGIVENESS AFTER REPAYMENT PERIOD:
- Remaining balance forgiven after 20-25 years of qualifying payments
- IMPORTANT: Forgiven amount may be taxable as ordinary income
- Example: $50,000 forgiven in high-income year = potentially $15,000+ in taxes
- Exception: PSLF forgiveness is NOT taxable

INTEREST ACCRUAL ON IDR PLANS:
- If your payment doesn't cover all accrued interest, balance can grow
- Called "negative amortization" - balance increases over time
- REPAYE helps: government pays 50% of unpaid subsidized loan interest
- At the end of repayment period, even large balances are forgiven

IDR PLAN COMPARISON TABLE:
Plan     | Rate  | Duration      | Cap?  | Interest Subsidy

IBR      | 10%   | 20-25 years   | Yes   | No

HOW TO ENROLL:
1. Go to StudentLoans.gov
2. Select "Income-Driven Repayment Plan Request"
3. Link your tax return (IRS Data Retrieval Tool)
4. Choose plan (or let system suggest best plan)
5. Confirm enrollment with loan servicer
6. Recertify annually

BEST PLAN FOR YOUR SITUATION:
- Pursuing PSLF: REPAYE or PAYE (lowest payments = most forgiven)
- High income: IBR (payment cap protects from high payments)
- Grad school loans: ICR (only option for PLUS loans)
- Maximum interest subsidy: REPAYE
- Recently graduated, low income: Any IDR plan (start with $0 payments if needed)
