CURRENT FEDERAL STUDENT LOAN INTEREST RATES (2024-2025)

FEDERAL DIRECT LOANS:
- Direct Subsidized Loans: 6.53% (fixed)
- Direct Unsubsidized Loans: 7.05% (fixed)
- Direct PLUS Loans: 8.05% (fixed)
- Direct Consolidation Loans: Weighted average of consolidated loans (rounded up to nearest 1/8%)

HOW INTEREST IS CALCULATED:
- Interest accrues daily on unsubsidized loans
- Interest does NOT accrue on subsidized loans while in school
- Formula: Loan Amount × Interest Rate ÷ 365 = Daily Interest
- Example: $10,000 loan at 6.53% = $1.79 daily interest

MONTHLY PAYMENT IMPACT OF INTEREST RATES:
- $30,000 loan at 6.53% (10-year repayment): $357/month total interest = $12,960
- $30,000 loan at 7.05% (10-year repayment): $368/month total interest = $13,780
- $30,000 loan at 8.05% (10-year repayment): $388/month total interest = $15,560

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
- 2023-2024: 6.53% (Subsidized), 7.05% (Unsubsidized), 8.05% (PLUS)
- 2022-2023: 3.73%, 5.28%, 6.28% (lower due to COVID freeze carryover)
- Rates change annually based on 10-year Treasury note + fixed margin
- Rates set by Congress, not by Department of Education
- All federal loans have FIXED rates (don't change over life of loan)

PRIVATE LOAN RATES (VARIABLE):
- Range: 3.0% to 13.0%+ depending on:
  - Credit score
  - Debt-to-income ratio
  - Cosigner status
  - Lender policies
  - Market conditions
- Some private loans have variable rates (can increase over time)
- Some have fixed rates

ORIGINATION FEES (2024-2025):
- Direct Subsidized Loans: 1.066%
- Direct Unsubsidized Loans: 1.066%
- Direct PLUS Loans: 4.228%
- Fee is deducted from loan disbursement

INTEREST RATE DIFFERENCES EXPLAINED:
- Subsidized loans lowest: Government pays interest in school
- Unsubsidized loans higher: Student responsible for all interest
- PLUS loans highest: Higher risk (parents/grad students), higher cost
- Private loans vary: Market-based, credit-dependent

WHEN INTEREST STARTS ACCRUING:
- Subsidized: After 6-month grace period post-graduation
- Unsubsidized: From loan disbursement date (even while in school)
- PLUS: From loan disbursement date
- Interest on unsubsidized can capitalize (adds to principal) if unpaid
